# Cascade Ascendant — Editorial Overlay v1

## Purpose of This Document

This overlay is designed to transform revision from an open-ended emotional process into a structured editorial workflow.

The goal is not to endlessly rewrite the manuscript.
The goal is to:

- identify scene function,
- isolate pacing issues,
- preserve thematic signal,
- reduce redundancy,
- improve tonal contrast,
- protect character voice,
- and create targeted refinement passes.

Each scene should be revised independently according to its specific editorial needs.

---

# REVISION PRINCIPLES

## Preserve

- Philosophical conviction
- Emotional authenticity
- Symbolic continuity
- Dominic/Sam/Astra emotional architecture
- Tom’s evolving identity arc
- The manuscript’s human core

## Reduce

- Emotional overstatement
- Repeated atmospheric phrasing
- Consecutive introspective beats
- Redundant thematic restatements
- Excessive tonal uniformity

## Increase

- Tonal elasticity
- Physical grounding
- Character voice differentiation
- Momentum between emotional peaks
- Strategic humor and friction

---

## PASS 1 — STRUCTURAL
- Scene necessity

- Strong thematic cohesion
- Sophisticated emotional architecture
- Distinct philosophical identity
- Excellent symbolic continuity
- Strong Dominic/Sam/Astra dynamic
- Literary cyberpunk tone
- Strong atmosphere

## Highest Revision Priorities

2. Compression of repeated emotional beats
3. Increase tonal elasticity
4. Character voice separation
5. Reduce atmospheric repetition

With controlled compression and tonal balancing, the manuscript could move from “strong ambitious SF manuscript” into “professionally competitive philosophical cyberpunk.”
